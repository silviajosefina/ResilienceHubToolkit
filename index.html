Resilience Hub Site Feasibility Toolkit

Comparative Site Assessment Framework — Score and compare candidate resilience hub locations

This toolkit provides a standardized, auditable framework for comparing candidate resilience hub sites across physical resilience, energy systems, community alignment, and financial viability. It is designed to support decision-making by community partners, planners, and technical teams evaluating which buildings are best suited to serve as resilience hubs during climate emergencies, power outages, and other disruptions.

Use the Site Comparison tab to score and compare candidate sites. Use the Siting Methodology tab for guidance on data collection approaches by category.

How to Use This Toolkit

  • 1
    Identify Candidate SitesCompile a list of candidate buildings through partner networks, community input, and local knowledge. Document baseline site information using the Site Intake form.
  • 2
    Collect DataConduct site walkthroughs, community engagement, and desk-based research in parallel. Use the Siting Methodology tab for guidance on what to collect for each scoring category.
  • 3
    Score Each SiteEnter scores in the Site Assessment tool using the standardized 0–5 scale. All scores should be traceable to a documented data source or observation.
  • 4
    Compare ResultsReview the Site Comparison table to evaluate sites across all four categories. Note red flags that may override composite scores.
  • 5
    Identify Top CandidatesUse composite scores, category breakdowns, and red flag status to recommend sites for next-stage engineering, financial, and stakeholder review.

Guidance on recommended data collection approaches for each scoring category. This page will be updated as the team develops and validates methodology.

Physical Resilience
  • PropertyShark or county assessor — ownership, building age, permits
  • Site walkthroughs with a licensed contractor
  • Establish a contractor relationship early for consistent assessments across sites
Community Alignment
  • Resident interviews and focus groups
  • Outreach through local community organizations
  • Conversations with community leaders and trusted intermediaries
Energy Systems
  • Engage local Community Choice Aggregator (e.g. Peninsula Clean Energy)
  • Document existing electrical infrastructure during site walkthrough
  • Use reasonable assumptions where metered data is unavailable
Financial Viability
  • Identify key costs from Physical Resilience and Energy Systems assessments
  • Apply a financial modeling tool to estimate upgrade and operating costs
  • Cross-reference with available funding programs and grant eligibility
