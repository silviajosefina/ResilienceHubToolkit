What is a Resilience Hub?

A resilience hub is a trusted community facility — a church, community center, school, or similar space — retrofitted to serve as a refuge and resource center before, during, and after climate-related disasters.

During an emergency, hubs provide cooling or warming, phone charging, water, food, and information to residents who need them most. Between emergencies, they continue their regular community function. The goal of this toolkit is to assess which candidate sites are most feasible and impactful as resilience hubs — accounting for physical condition, community trust, energy upgrade potential, and financial viability.
